Yamaha YZF-R9

The Yamaha YZF-R9, announced for 2025, represents a significant evolution in Yamaha's supersport lineup, bridging the gap between the YZF-R7 and the flagship YZF-R1.

The YZF-R9 is a brand-new addition to Yamaha's R-Series, designed to deliver performance and accessibility for riders seeking a mid-tier supersport motorcycle. Drawing inspiration from MotoGP technology and Yamaha’s racing heritage, the R9 combines aggressive styling with advanced engineering to appeal to both track enthusiasts and street riders.

Introduction and History

The R9 combines cutting-edge technology and Yamaha’s racing expertise to offer a mid-range supersport option that stands out in performance and style. With its aggressive design and advanced engineering, it’s built for both the street and the track.

Specifications

  • Engine Type: 890cc, liquid-cooled, 3-cylinder, DOHC, 4-valve.
  • Powertrain: Yamaha's Chip Controlled Throttle (YCC-T®) delivers precise control.
  • Transmission: 6-speed constant mesh with assist and slipper clutch.
  • Quickshifter: Third-generation system for clutchless upshifts and downshifts.
  • Frame: Lightweight aluminum Deltabox for improved handling.
  • Suspension: Fully adjustable KYB forks (front) and linked suspension (rear).
  • Brakes: Dual 320mm Brembo Stylema discs (front) and a 220mm disc (rear) with ABS.
  • Dimensions: Wet weight: 195 kg; Seat height: 831mm.
  • Tires: Front: 120/70 ZR17; Rear: 180/55 ZR17.
  • Fuel Tank: 14 liters capacity.

Electronics and Features

  • 6-axis IMU for traction, slide, lift, and brake control.
  • Yamaha Ride Control (YRC) with customizable modes.
  • GPS-integrated Y-TRAC telemetry for real-time performance tracking.
  • Full-color 5-inch TFT display.
  • Launch control, cruise control, and full LED lighting.

Pricing and Availability

The Yamaha YZF-R9 is priced at $12,499 and will be available in three stunning color options: Team Yamaha Blue, Matte Raven Black, and Intensity White/Redline. Global dealerships will begin offering the bike in mid-2025.

Conclusion

With its advanced electronics, powerful engine, and race-ready chassis, the Yamaha YZF-R9 is set to become a standout option in the supersport category, catering to riders who want a balance between performance, technology, and style.
